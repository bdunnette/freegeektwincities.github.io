We are now accepting computer donations! Drop off your donations on during our normal posted hours at 2537 25th Ave S. in the Seward neighborhood of Minneapolis.

Our program begins with collecting computers and other electronics. We use these materials to build new computers or recycle the materials responsibly. You can be certain that your old computer parts are getting the most use out of the or are being recycled in the best way possible. We do suggest that you donate some money with your hardware donation so that we can process the hardware properly. All donations are tax deductible.
  • Computers ($5.00 ea.)
  • Printers ($4.00 ea.)
  • Scanners ($3.00 ea.)
  • LCD monitors ($4.00 ea.)
  • CRT monitors (mandatory $20.00 ea., due to high recycling costs for CRTs!)

  • Networking Devices ($1.00 ea.)
  • Upgrade parts, Memory, DVD Devices.
  • Telephones, including Cell Phones ($2.00 ea.)
  • Cameras, Video equipment, audio equipment, lighting ($2.00 ea.)
  • Video Game Consoles, Electronic Toys and Games,  Electronic children's devices ($1.00 ea.)
  • UPS ($5.00 ea.)
  • MP3, Walkman, Compact Disc, DVD players
  • Cables, Misc Computer accessories, and Electronic Toys, video games.
  • Small household appliances such as blenders, vacuum cleaners, microwaves, and any of those broken late night infomercial specials.
This list isn’t exhaustive! If you have a piece of equipment and you’re wondering whether we take it, you might want to guide yourself by the list of what we don’t take below.

We DO NOT Accept

  • Styrofoam
  • Electronics or appliances that have chemicals such as RF-13a (Air Conditioners, De-Humidifiers, etc)
  • Smoke Detectors
  • Large Household Appliances (including air conditioners)
  • Batteries
  • Fluorescent Light Bulbs
  • Televisions
Please see Rethink Recycling for a comprehensive list of places to donate these items.

How To Donate Hardware

Please bring as little packaging and boxes as possible. Please recycle the boxes for your equipment through your curb-side recycling program. We do not accept Styrofoam. We accept all software, manuals, and cables for donation. While we can’t promise we’ll use them all, we do recycle them responsibly if we can’t make use of them. Recycling computers is labor intensive. Doing any of the following to prepare your equipment for donation will help us speed the processing and recycling of your equipment.
  1. Untangle all cords and cables. Coil them up with a rubber band or twist tie.
  2. Attach power adapters (AC adaptors, transformers) to their associated equipment (such as powered speakers, printers or routers) as best you can.
